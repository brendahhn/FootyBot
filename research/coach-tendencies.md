# Coach & scheme tendencies — 2026 season

Status: expanded 2026-07-01 (first pass 2026-06-30). Covers the highest fantasy-impact coaching
changes from the 2026 offseason cycle (confirmed via WebSearch), not all 32 teams yet. 2026-07-01
runs added Chargers, Titans, Falcons, Buccaneers, and (later same day) a full **Eagles** entry
(Sirianni retained, Sean Mannion new first-time playcaller — directly serves Brendan's stated
Saquon-at-#4 interest), all A-tier sourcing; and re-verified the Ravens and Browns entries against
fresh sources after a garbled search summary tried to swap them (see notebook CHANGELOG 2026-07-01). WebFetch and direct
data pulls are blocked in this environment (see `CONTEXT.md` open questions / the pipeline
blocker), so this is built from search-result snippets plus established scheme knowledge for
each coach's track record — treat specifics as directional, not exact, and re-verify closer
to the draft once preseason snaps give real 2026 data.

Methodology going forward: for each team with a new playcaller, note (1) who was hired and
from where, (2) their scheme lineage/tree, (3) known tendencies (run scheme: zone vs. gap;
shotgun vs. under center rate; pace; pass-game shape: play-action/explosive vs. short/rhythm),
(4) fantasy-relevant implication for this team's skill players. Update as preseason/Week 1-4
data becomes available.

## Teams with new offensive playcallers (highest fantasy relevance)

### Las Vegas Raiders — HC Klint Kubiak (calls plays), OC Andrew Janocko
- Background: Kubiak is from the Kubiak coaching family/tree (zone-blocking running game),
  most recently Seahawks OC, before that Saints OC and 49ers passing coordinator.
- Tendencies: heavy outside/wide-zone run scheme; notably still uses a **fullback** in
  multiple sets (rare league-wide) — Seattle was near the top of the league in fullback
  personnel usage under Kubiak in 2025. Seattle's rush offense jumped from 28th to ~10th in
  yards under his playcalling.
- Fantasy implication: zone-run backs benefit from one-cut, outside-zone gap reads (favors
  vision/burst over pure power); positive signal for Ashton Jeanty's role; fullback usage
  means snaps for an FB and slightly fewer 11-personnel passing-down looks than a pure
  spread team.

### Arizona Cardinals — HC Mike LaFleur (calls plays)
- Background: McVay/Kyle Shanahan coaching tree — 3 years with McVay (Rams), 7 years with
  Shanahan (49ers) before this.
- Tendencies: heavy pre-snap shifts/motion, timing-and-route-running-dependent passing game,
  outside-zone run scheme, establish-the-run-to-set-up-play-action philosophy. Explicitly
  different from predecessor Drew Petzing's downhill/gap run scheme — this is a real scheme
  change, not just a coordinator title swap.
- Fantasy implication: classic Shanahan-tree WR profile (route-runners who win via leverage/
  motion, not just pure separation speed) gets a boost; zone-run backs preferred over power
  backs; play-action-heavy passing boosts QB/WR efficiency stats but adds variance.

### Cleveland Browns — HC Todd Monken (calls plays)
- Background: Ravens OC (3 years) before this, Georgia OC (national title), Air Raid roots
  (Jaguars WR coach background) blended with power-run elements at his more recent stops.
- Tendencies: explicitly adapts scheme to roster talent rather than imposing a fixed system;
  uses motion, tempo, and pre-snap structure to simplify QB reads and create defensive
  conflict; "multiple" scheme — not a pure Air Raid throw-first system despite his roots.
- Fantasy implication: less predictable scheme fit than most coaches on this list — his
  Ravens tenure was run-heavy/explosive-pass-play-driven (paired with a mobile QB); Browns
  personnel will shape this more than dogma will. Re-evaluate once Cleveland's QB situation
  and personnel usage is clear in camp.

### Buffalo Bills — HC Joe Brady (calls plays), OC Pete Carmichael Jr.
- Background: Brady is Sean Payton coaching-tree; hired Carmichael, Payton's longtime top
  lieutenant (Saints OC for many years), as his OC.
- Tendencies: Payton-tree offenses historically pass more than Buffalo did under the previous
  staff, use heavier personnel groupings, and lean on under-center play-action for explosive
  shots — a contrast with Buffalo's 2025 identity as the league's top rushing offense. Brady
  has said he'll fit the system to the roster ("you don't make players fit a system").
- Fantasy implication: watch early-season pass/run mix closely — if Carmichael's Payton-style
  system actually displaces some of James Cook's 2025 rushing-title workload toward more
  balanced/under-center play-action passing, that's a material change to his profile and to
  the Bills' receivers' target share.

### Baltimore Ravens — HC Jesse Minter (defensive background, ex-Chargers DC), OC Declan Doyle
- Background: Minter is a defensive-minded HC (first-time HC), so the offensive scheme is
  Doyle's — Doyle is a first-time NFL playcaller (no prior playcalling experience), making
  this the hardest 2026 offense to project from track record alone.
- Fantasy implication: low confidence on scheme tendencies until real 2026 snaps exist;
  flag for re-evaluation in camp/preseason rather than projecting off history.

### Pittsburgh Steelers — HC Mike McCarthy
- Background: long track record (Packers, Cowboys) as a West Coast offense traditionalist.
- Tendencies: historically more under-center, rhythm/timing-based passing, ball-control
  run game relative to the current shotgun-spread league average.
- Fantasy implication: under-center, rhythm-passing offenses tend to spread targets more
  evenly among possession-type WRs/TEs and depress big-play (40+) bonus production relative
  to spread/shotgun teams — relevant given this league's 40+ yard bonus scoring.

### Miami Dolphins — HC Jeff Hafley (defensive background, ex-Packers DC), OC Bobby Slowik
- Background: Slowik was Miami's senior pass-game coordinator in 2025, promoted to OC; he's
  originally from the McVay/Shanahan tree (former Texans OC).
- Tendencies: expect continuity with Shanahan-tree zone-run/motion principles given Slowik's
  background, but he's now the lead voice rather than executing someone else's system —
  worth re-confirming once 2026 play-calling patterns are visible.

### Los Angeles Chargers — OC Mike McDaniel (calls plays), HC Jim Harbaugh
- Background (A): McDaniel is the fired Dolphins HC (2022-2025), hired as Chargers OC/playcaller
  after Harbaugh fired Greg Roman following the 2025 playoff loss (sourced: chargers.com,
  NFL.com, 2026). HC Harbaugh is not new (since 2024); the *playcaller* is. McDaniel is
  Kyle Shanahan tree — wide/outside-zone run game, heavy pre-snap motion, YAC-manufacturing
  short passing.
- Tendencies (A on lineage; B on how it blends here): the open question the sources themselves
  flag is the collision between McDaniel's motion-heavy zone system and Harbaugh's stated
  run-first, under-center identity. McDaniel is described as "adding his flavor" to Harbaugh's
  run-first mindset, and the stated hiring rationale was protecting Herbert (scheme him easier
  throws), not turning LA into a pass-funnel. So do NOT assume a Miami-level pass rate — the
  pass/run mix is genuinely uncertain until camp.
- Fantasy implication (B): positive for Justin Herbert efficiency (McDaniel's scheme
  manufactures open throws / YAC) — but with volume uncertainty from Harbaugh's run lean, this
  is an efficiency-up, not necessarily attempts-up, story. Motion/YAC scheme favors a
  separation-and-YAC slot/underneath receiver profile (e.g. Ladd McConkey archetype) more than
  a pure contested-catch boundary type. Wide-zone backfield: vision/one-cut backs benefit;
  watch for a committee, which is common in this scheme.

### Tennessee Titans — HC Robert Saleh (defensive background, ex-49ers DC), OC Brian Daboll
- Background (A): Saleh (ex-49ers DC, ex-Jets HC) is defensive-minded, so the offense is
  Daboll's. Daboll is the fired Giants HC and a well-established QB developer (sourced:
  tennesseetitans.com, ESPN, NFL.com, 2026). This pairs a first-time-in-Tennessee scheme with
  a 2nd-year QB (Cam Ward, 2025 #1 overall).
- Tendencies (A on Daboll's track record; B applied to this roster): Daboll offenses lean on
  play-action with vertical/downfield concepts and RPO elements — a fit noted for Ward's
  college (Miami) RPO background. QB-developer reputation is real (Josh Allen's ascent).
- Fantasy implication (B, with a Speculative tail): the bull case is a Cam Ward Year-2 leap
  under a QB-developer OC — but a 2nd-year-QB breakout is a projection, NOT a lean to bank on;
  the 2025 Titans offense was among the league's worst, and a new scheme + young QB is
  high-variance. Vertical/play-action shape is friendly to this league's 40+ yard bonuses IF
  the offense takes a step forward. Treat any Titans skill-player call as upside-with-variance,
  not floor. Re-evaluate once preseason shows whether the O-line/protection actually lets the
  downfield concepts function.

### Atlanta Falcons — HC Kevin Stefanski, OC Tommy Rees (calls plays)
- Background (A): Stefanski is the fired Browns HC (2020-2025, 2x Coach of the Year), hired as
  Falcons HC; he brought Tommy Rees as OC and — importantly — has said **Rees calls the plays**,
  the same division of labor they ran in Cleveland in 2025 (sourced: atlantafalcons.com,
  Bleacher Report, NFL.com, 2026). Common mistake to avoid: assuming Stefanski himself calls
  plays. Stefanski's system is outside/wide-zone rushing, condensed formations, heavy
  play-action and 12-personnel (2-TE) usage.
- Tendencies (A on scheme identity): wide-zone bell-cow run scheme with bootleg/play-action off
  it; historically elevates a lead back's rushing workload and efficiency.
- Fantasy implication (A/B): strong scheme fit for **Bijan Robinson** as a wide-zone, cut-back
  bell cow — the most quotable positive of this hire, and Stefanski publicly courted him. Caveat
  to keep honest: Bijan was already a heavy-usage back, so the delta is scheme-efficiency and
  potential rushing-share consolidation, not "discovering" volume; and a run-heavier,
  12-personnel identity could *cap* team pass volume, a mild negative for the WR room's ceiling.
  QB is a real swing (Penix vs. Cousins situation referenced in sources) — leave that thread
  open until camp.

### Tampa Bay Buccaneers — OC Zac Robinson (new playcaller), HC Todd Bowles
- Background (A): Bucs fired 2025 first-year OC Josh Grizzard (offense fell from ~3rd in 2024 to
  ~21st, scoring 28.6 → 21.4 PPG) and hired Zac Robinson, the Falcons' 2024-2025 OC and a McVay
  tree product (ex-Rams QB coach/passing-game coordinator). Baker Mayfield reportedly pushed for
  the hire from a prior LA relationship (sourced: buccaneers.com, ESPN, NFL.com, 2026). HC Bowles
  is not new; the playcaller is.
- Tendencies (A on lineage): McVay-tree — pre-snap motion, condensed splits, play-action off
  wide zone, timing-based route structure.
- Fantasy implication (B) + cross-reference: this is a genuine **scheme change** in Tampa, which
  matters for the Emeka Egbuka breakout comp in `research/breakout-comps.md` — that comp's
  thesis was "no scheme change required, just more snaps." A new OC installing a new system
  weakens that specific assumption (new scheme = new usage patterns, and rookie-year efficiency
  in one system doesn't transfer 1:1). See the updated failure-mode note there.

### Philadelphia Eagles — HC Nick Sirianni (retained), OC Sean Mannion (new playcaller)
- Background (A): This is an **OC-only change, not a regime change** — Sirianni stays HC. The
  Eagles fired 2025 OC/playcaller Kevin Patullo after one stagnant season (Hurts hit fewer big
  plays; the passing game went cold for long stretches) and hired **Sean Mannion**, Green Bay's
  QB coach the past two seasons. Critical caveat: **Mannion has never called plays at any level —
  a true first-time playcaller learning on the job** on a Super-Bowl-expectation roster (sourced:
  NFL.com, NBC Sports Philadelphia, Yahoo Sports, ESPN, 2026). He is Sirianni's 5th OC and Hurts'
  7th play caller.
- Tendencies (B — reported philosophy, NOT an observed track record): Sirianni has said Mannion
  will install **more zone-blocking run concepts** and more **under-center** work; reporting
  places his philosophy closer to the Shanahan/McVay/Kubiak tree than Sirianni's prior
  shotgun-spread identity. Tagged B, not A, deliberately: with zero playcalling sample, these are
  stated intentions + lineage inference, not tendencies anyone has watched him call. 2025 RB-run
  volume was 21.9/gm (2nd-lowest of the Sirianni era, down from 26.7 in 2024) and is widely
  expected to normalize toward league-middle.
- Fantasy implication — **Saquon Barkley** (the draft-relevant one; Brendan flagged wanting a
  Saquon + Chase pairing at pick #4):
  - The under-center shift is **not** a negative for him. Media-cited 2024 splits: **6.1 YPC
    under center vs. 5.6 in shotgun**, with ~half of his 2,005 rushing yards coming from under
    center (A on the split being reported; NOT pipeline-computed).
  - 2025 was a genuine down year: 1,140 rush yds / 7 TD / 4.1 YPC on 280 carries, vs. 2024's
    2,005 / 5.8 YPC / 345 carries (A: CBS, SI). Honest correlation-vs-causation read: his
    **yards before contact collapsed 3.8 → 1.7** — that implicates blocking/scheme decline under
    Patullo as much as age, which is precisely why new zone concepts *could* help. But he turns
    29 in 2026, so age-related decline is a live competing explanation, not one to wave away.
  - **Net (B / Speculative on the upside):** the rebound mechanism is real (new zone scheme +
    likely volume normalization + under-center fits his profile), but a "major bounceback" rests
    on a first-time playcaller actually executing. Treat Saquon as a strong RB1 on talent/role —
    not as a bet on the scheme change delivering a return to the 2024 ceiling. Don't pay a
    2024-ceiling price.
- Failure modes (what keeps this entry honest):
  1. **First-time-playcaller risk** — Mannion has never called an NFL play; the projected scheme
     shape may not materialize cleanly in year one on a high-pressure roster.
  2. **Hurts/scheme friction** — Hurts reportedly pushed back on going under center more and has
     been described as having "rigid preferences." If the QB resists, the under-center/zone shift
     gets diluted. (Relevant to this league's **6pt passing TDs**: Hurts' value leans on rushing
     TDs plus whatever passing-volume/efficiency bump this scheme is supposed to unlock — so this
     friction cuts at his QB ceiling specifically.)
- Sources (WebSearch, 2026-07-01, all A-tier outlets): NFL.com (Mannion hire), NBC Sports
  Philadelphia (readiness/first-time-playcaller), ESPN ("evil genius" scheme profile), CBS Sports
  (Hurts "rigid preferences"; Saquon 2025 decline + bounceback framing), SI/onsi (Saquon
  yards-before-contact regression), Philadelphia Inquirer (under-center debate, Barkley YPC
  splits, RB-run-rate trend).

## Notable non-change (don't over-read into "new regime" narratives)

### Jacksonville Jaguars — HC Liam Coen, OC Grant Udinski, DC Anthony Campanile all retained
2025 was a franchise-record offensive season (474 points, 55 TDs) under this staff; **no
coaching change here for 2026.** Earlier sourcing for this doc incorrectly suggested a Jaguars
HC change (conflated with Jesse Minter, who went to Baltimore, not Jacksonville) — flagging
this explicitly since it's exactly the kind of bad-data risk this project needs to guard
against. Treat Jacksonville as scheme-stable; project off 2025 tape directly.

### Kansas City Chiefs — OC Eric Bieniemy returns
Bieniemy replaces Matt Nagy (who left to become Bears OC) and previously ran Kansas City's
offense for 5 seasons (2018-2022) under Andy Reid. This is a return to a previously-successful
system, not a new scheme — lower projection uncertainty than the other changes on this list.
